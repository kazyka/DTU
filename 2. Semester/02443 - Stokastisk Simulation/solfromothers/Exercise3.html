Exponential distribution¶

The time between events in a Poisson process is exponentially distributed. (Arrival time) \begin{equation} F(x)=1−e(−λx)\quad E(X)= \frac{1}{\lambda}\quad F^{−1}(u)=−\frac{1}{\lambda}log(1−u) \end{equation} So (both $1 − U$ and $U$ is uniform distributed) $X = \frac{−log(U)}{\lambda} ∼ exp(λ)$

The exponential distribution is thus generated as:

Pareto¶

Pareto is often used in connection to description of income (over a certain level). \begin{equation} \mathbf{X}∼Pa(k,β)\quad \mathbf{F}(x)=1−\left(\frac{\beta}{x}\right)^k\quad x\geq \beta\quad \mathbf{X}=\beta(U^{-\frac{1}{k}}) \end{equation} \begin{equation} E(X)=\frac{1}{1-k}\beta\quad V(k)=\frac{k}{(k-1)^2(k-2)}\beta^2\quad k>1,2 \end{equation} Pareto $\mathbf{X}\geq 0$ \begin{equation} F(X)=1-\left(1+\frac{x}{\beta}\right)^{-k}\quad X=β(􏰂U^{-\frac{1}{k}}−1􏰃) \end{equation}

Gaussian¶

$X$ a result of many $(∞)$ independent sources (Central limit theorem) \begin{equation} X ∼ N(μ,σ2) \end{equation} \begin{equation} Z ∼ N(0, 1) \quad X ∼ N(μ,σ2) \quad X = μ + σZ \quad Z = Φ−1(U) \end{equation}

The Box-Muller method A transformation from polar $(θ = 2πU2, r = 􏰉\sqrt{−2 log(U1))}$ into cartisan \begin{equation} \begin{bmatrix} Z1 \\ Z2\end{bmatrix}= 􏰉􏰉\sqrt{−2 log(U1))} \begin{bmatrix}cos(2πU2)\\sin(2πU2) \end{bmatrix} \end{equation}

meanArr = []
varArr = []
for _ in range(50):
    U = np.random_intel.rand(10)
    U2 = np.random_intel.rand(10)
    Z =np.multiply(np.sqrt([-2*np.log(U)]),np.array([np.cos(2*math.pi*U2),np.sin(2*math.pi*U2)]))
    meanArr.append(Z[0].mean())
    meanArr.append(Z[1].mean())
    varArr.append(Z[0].var())
    varArr.append(Z[1].var())

meanArr = np.array(meanArr)
varArr = np.array(varArr)
fig,ax = plt.subplots(1,figsize=(15,5))
ax.plot(meanArr,label="$\hat{\\theta}$")
ax.plot(meanArr+(varArr/10)**(1/2)*1.96,'--',c='r')
ax.plot(meanArr-(varArr/10)**(1/2)*1.96,'--',c='r',label="$5\%$ CI")
